Want to learn how to use the website? Then you've come to the right place!

The Log-In Screen:

It's pretty self-explanatory, and easy to understand if you have ever used a computer before. Just type in your username and password and then press "enter", or click the "submit" button. You won't be able to sign in if you don't provide a registered username and password. Funny messages remind you to do that.

If you don't have an account, then click the "No account? Click here to make one!" link to make one. If you have an account but need to change your password for some reason, click the "Forgot your password? Click here to get a new one!" link.

*Please use the app buttons for navigation. If you use the browser forward and backward buttons for navigation, you lose your session data. You would have to sign into the app all over again.

The Sign-Up Sheet:

For those who don't have an account, the sign-up sheet webpage exists to help you register an account on the website. You just enter your username, email account, password (twice to make sure you got it right), and a special passcode to help you change your password if necessary. You cannot make your account until all information is filled out. Funny messages remind you to do that.

*Remember: keep your passcode, password, and even your username safe. With today's cyber-threats, social engineering and especially AI technology, hackers could steal your information more easily than you think.

The New Password Page:

We prefer that you remember your password, or write it down and keep it somewhere safe. However, we understand that there are times you need to change it. (For example, either you forgot it, someone stole it, or your friend used it to steal your vacation ideas for the group.) In that case, this page exists to help you out.

You would need to enter your email, username, your new password twice, and most importantly, your passcode. That passcode is what enables you to change your password. You should keep that even more safe than your password. However, if you ever need to change it, call this number: (111) 222-3333. And remember, you can't change your password if anything in the form is missing. Funny messages will remind you of that.

The Vacation List Page:

Now you are really using the app. This is where you will build your vacation plan. Initially, it is empty for first-time users. There is a button below called "Add Vacation?" That takes users to a page where they can start creating their first vacation plan. As users add vacations, the "Vacation List" page will fill up with boxes having the vacation names on them. Clicking on them shows the user the vacation details. Those details can be updated when needed. (Maybe users need to make some last-minute changes to their plans in this crazy world?)

There is a "Search" button on the page that enables users to search for vacations by their title. You can start typing the vacation title into the box, or a word in the title, and press "Search". If any vacation has the title, or that word in the title, then the app shows it. If not, the app shows no vacations. To see all vacations, press "Search" with an empty box.

*Note, the Search functionality is case-sensitive.

The Vacation Details Page:

This is the "meat" of the burger. The main part of the app. Drum-roll please: ... The Vacation Details Page! Here, you can actually build your vacation plans.

You start by entering your title, like all meaningful vacations have. Then, you enter the place you plan on staying in, preferably somewhere cheap so you can spend more money on your adventures. Then you enter your vacation start-date. (Here's a tip, factor in the time it takes for you to reach your hotel, then some rest before you explore.) Next, enter your vacation end-date. (Another tip, factor in the time it takes to pack your stuff, drive back home, and wake up the next day, ready for work. It might just save your career.) Finally, make a short description of your vacation. It reminds you of what it's about, and it helps give you something to look forward to during the tiring workweek.

There is also a list of the vacation's excursions below. The list is empty if the vacation has no excursions. However, there is a button below called "Add Excursions?" that takes users to a page where they can create excursions for the vacation. As excursions are added to a vacation, they appear as a list of blocks with excursion names. Clicking on a block takes the user to a page that shows the excursion's details, which can be edited.

Users can search for a vacation's excursions by title. You can type words and press the search button to find all excursions that match the text. If users press the search button with an empty text-box, all the vacation's excursions are shown.

*Note, the Search functionality is case-sensitive.

If you are done with a vacation or you change plans, you can delete the vacation, but not if it has excursions.

The Excursion Details Page:

This page tells you the details of a vacation's particular excursion. It also allows you to make a vacation's new excursion. If you are planning a new excursion, It should have these things.

First, the title. Choose a memorable, exciting excursion title.

Second, the start-date. Since you only have so many vacation days, the excursion will probably last one day. (Unless it lasts multiple days, then you should make multiple excursions.) You should keep track of when the excursion happens.

Third,the description. Since this is a specific event in an overall vacation, It should have more detail than a vacation's description. In addition, you should do your research about a specific excursion. What if the place has thieves, floods, earthquakes, and high-priced beverages? Be careful and considerate in your vacation description.

All excursions have a "Delete Excursion?" button to delete the excursion if it ended, or if plans have changed.

The Profile Page:

This page shows users their personal information on the site, including their id, username, and email. You can change the username and email by writing new ones, then clicking the save button.

The profile page also has a "Delete Account" button for deleting a person's account on the app. We don't know why you would want to do that, but we realize that some people would want that ability. (Perhaps unless your brother got your passcode or account info and started messing up everything.)

*If you don't see your data on the pages at anytime, try using the in-app back buttons to return to the previous page, then access the page again.
